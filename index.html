Current Research Project Working Paper (Available upon request)

A Cross-Dose-Response Function: Mortality-Temperature Relationship with Temperature Quantile

This paper introduces a Cross-Dose-Response Function (CDRF) model that reconciles rich meteorological data with socioeconomic availability. By mapping hourly temperature quantiles to monthly mortality, I mitigate aggregation bias while circumventing data privacy restrictions associated with daily time-series models.

Figure: Income DRF (from paper)

Climate Inequality

Adaptation is strictly constrained by economic capacity. A 25% increase in income reduces mortality risk by approximately 10% across all temperatures.

Figure: Age DRF (from paper)

Demographic Risk

Aging populations face disproportionate risks. A 3-year increase in county mean age correlates with a ~25% higher mortality rate, regardless of temperature.

Figure: Quantile Coverage (from paper)

Method Innovation

Utilized temperature quantiles to internalize local acclimatization. This allows for robust semi-parametric estimation using public monthly data instead of restricted daily files.

  • Implementation: Processed nearly 40 years of hourly weather data (1982-2019) across 403 U.S. counties using custom Python & GPU computing pipelines.
